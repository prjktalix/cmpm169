Integrate / Innovate pt.1

The integration and innovation part is something I struggled with for this assignment. Both steps overlap, and my goal is to integrate them all. I believe the innovative part involves bringing the filter to reality, as this will be the most challenging aspect. With that in mind, I want to separate the steps into two parts: the integration part involves the creation of the snow and the sound art, and the filter falls under the innovation step.

My script was inspired by Wes Modes' 'Snowy Painting.' I used both scripts from sketch.js and snowflake.js to create snowflakes on my webcam feed. I made changes in sketch.js by removing the paintings and any references to them. I also added webcam capture functionality in the setup function and modified the draw function to display the webcam feed instead of static images.

Then, I modified the snowflake.js script, adjusting the surface property collision to target my shoulders. Before implementing the collision check, I researched the eye filter mentioned above, involving poseNet and ml5.js, and found it very interesting. This is the first innovation I plan to use to make it work. You can find the link and the image example below. click here

Going back to the snowflake.js script, I modified my collision detection where the snowflakes hit my shoulder. It does work in some capacity, but it might be weird from my webcam position.

I tried adding sounds to the snowflake script where every snowflake that hit on my shoulder would create a popping sound, but it ended up a disaster where I heard a pop every millisecond, due to an excessive amount of snowflakes hitting my shoulder. If you want to hear the sound, you can uncomment the script line 88 in snowflake.js.

Reflection

I worked on this project by myself, and it was very fun to make. I primarily integrated the work my professor did, adding filters and implementing them on the webcam. I really wanted to recreate the filters found on Instagram, Snapchat, or TikTok, but the images were hard to find. My high point was definitely transitioning the work from images to the webcam. The ml5.js and pose estimation really helped my script work. The low point was the thought process of looking for ideas to make it work. I struggled to think of any ideas because the assignment's requirements to integrate and innovate overlapped a lot. That's why I asked my TA about it and clarified how I could make it work. In addition to my challenges, integrating sound art into my work was difficult. Adding sound did more harm than good, so I decided to comment out my pop.play just in case you want to hear how it sounds. Overall, I really had fun with this project!
